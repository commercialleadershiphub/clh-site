Executive Meeting Playbook

Prep, narrative and follow-up for CXO meetings that advance deals.

TL;DR

  • Outcome first: walk in with 1–2 concrete decisions you want, walk out with dated next steps.
  • One-page brief: account context, risks, asks, owners. Everyone reads it 24h before.
  • 45–60 min agenda: context (5) → their priorities (10) → value narrative (15) → plan (10) → decisions & dates (5–10).
  • Artifacts: 3 slides max + 1 customer story + a simple ROI frame. No demo unless it proves the decision.
  • Same-day follow-up: recap email + calendar holds + internal debrief in 30 min.

When to use this

  • Multi-threading into executive sponsors or aligning a buying committee.
  • Unblocking a stalled deal that needs an exec decision (budget, risk, timeline).
  • Converting proof to plan: moving from pilot/POC to commercial close.

Preparation (48–24 hours before)

One-page brief

  • Context: business objective, current state, key risks.
  • Stakeholders: decider, sponsors, evaluators, blockers.
  • Asks: the 1–2 decisions we want today.
  • Proof: 1 customer story + 2 metrics that matter to them.
  • Plan: 90-day outline with owners/dates.

Pre-wire

  • Align your champion on the narrative and the asks.
  • Send the agenda and brief for review. Ask, “What would make this a great use of time?”
  • Dry-run with your exec. Decide who will say what and when.

Suggested 45–60 min agenda

  1. Context & goals (5) — why we’re meeting now; confirm the decisions we’re hoping to make.
  2. Their priorities (10) — ask 3 focused questions; reflect back what matters.
  3. Value narrative (15) — outcomes, evidence, and the simplest ROI math they’ll accept.
  4. Proof & plan (10) — who does what, risk mitigations, timeline with gates.
  5. Decision & next steps (5–10) — owners, dates, comms. Put the dates on screen.

Tip: book 50 minutes, not 60, to leave breathing room and avoid getting cut off.

Narrative & slides (max 3)

  1. Today’s decision — 1–2 asks, why now, and the impact of delay.
  2. Outcomes with proof — customer story, 2 KPIs, and a simple ROI frame.
  3. Plan & risk — 90-day plan, owners, milestones, top risks with mitigations.

If you must demo, make it a 2-minute proof moment that de-risks the decision.

Asks that work (examples)

  • “Approve a 45-day pilot scoped to 2 teams with weekly checkpoints, starting Sept 2.”
  • “Name an executive sponsor and confirm procurement path so we can target a Nov 15 close.”
  • “Green-light the integration and security review next week so we don’t slip the launch.”

Follow-up (same day)

External recap (email)

Subject: Today’s decisions & next steps

Thanks for the time today. As agreed:
• Decisions: Pilot in Region North; sponsor: A. Jensen
• Risks & mitigations: data export → covered by connector; change mgmt → enablement plan attached
• Owners & dates: Kickoff 02/09 (you: B. Larsen, us: M. Khan). Midpoint 27/09. Commercial review 14/10.

Attaching the one-pager and the pilot plan. Shout if anything is off.

Internal debrief (30 min)

  • Were our asks clear? Did we earn them?
  • What surprised us (signal)? What do we change next time?
  • Update MEDDICC fields and next steps before leaving the room.

Risks & how to handle them

  • It turns into a demo: park the flow, promise a 15-min follow-up focused on their 2 risks.
  • No decision maker present: use the time to co-draft the exec email your champion will send.
  • Scope creep: restate the objective and timebox; suggest a phase-2 for extras.
  • Price objection too early: anchor outcomes and plan; promise a short pricing workshop next.

Metrics to track

  • % executive meetings that end with dated next steps (target: >80%)
  • Avg. days from exec meeting → commercial close
  • Pilot success rate when approved by an exec sponsor
  • Win rate when a CXO attends any meeting in the cycle

Artifacts (download list)

  • CXO one-pager template
  • Risk log (lightweight)
  • ROI frame (assumptions + sensitivity)
  • Pilot plan (45-day) with owners & dates

FAQ

Hvor mange deltagere? 5–7 max. Én beslutningstager, én sponsor, få specialister.

Hvad hvis vi ikke får beslutningen? Sigt efter en mindre beslutning: pilot, due diligence eller sponsor.
