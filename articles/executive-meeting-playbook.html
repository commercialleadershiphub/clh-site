Executive Meeting Playbook

Turn senior time into decisions. Script the storyline, pre-wire the room, show evidence, present options, ask clearly, and book the next milestone.

Introduction

Executive meetings fail when the ask is fuzzy and the path is unclear. Leaders do not need a feature tour; they need a storyline that links the problem to cost, shows the evidence, offers options with trade-offs and asks for specific decisions. This playbook gives you a proven structure and scripts so senior time produces momentum, not rework.

Use it for board prep, C-suite updates, large deal reviews and cross-functional decisions. The cadence is simple: pre-wire the room, send a one-page pre-read, open with a 90-second storyline, show 2–3 slides of evidence, present options, make 2–3 explicit asks and confirm the next milestone on the calendar. Then send a crisp recap within 24 hours.

What good looks like

  • Clarity: the first 90 seconds tell the story and the ask.
  • Evidence: numbers and references beat claims and opinions.
  • Options: two or three realistic paths with explicit trade-offs.
  • Decisions: owners, dates, calendar holds and status captured live.

Playbook

  1. Pre-wire: EA/Chief of Staff, finance and security; log concerns.
  2. Pre-read: one pager with problem cost, evidence, options, asks.
  3. Open: 90-second storyline. Ask permission to drive to decisions.
  4. Evidence: benchmarks, customer proof, risks and mitigations.
  5. Options & asks: show trade-offs; ask for 2–3 specific decisions.
  6. Next milestone: book date in the room; assign owners.
  7. Recap in 24h: decisions, actions, links and holds.
