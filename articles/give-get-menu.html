Give/Get Menu

Trade value for value. Guardrails first, then a simple worksheet to track live negotiations.

Introduction

Discounting is not a strategy. Trades are. A give/get menu sets clear boundaries, speeds approvals and protects margin without slowing the deal. It also improves confidence because reps know what is flexible and what is firm.

What good looks like

  • Guardrails: floors, term bands, approval lines.
  • Menu: pre-defined gives with costs and gets with value.
  • Live worksheet: track trades with owners and status.
  • Posture: hold the line when value is not returned.

Playbook

  1. Define floors and non-starters with Finance and Legal.
  2. List 6–10 common gives and matching gets.
  3. Publish approval paths and turnaround SLAs.
  4. Use the worksheet on every negotiation call.

Worked examples

Example A — Term for price

Get: 24-month term and reference. Give: price band within posture and flexible start date.

Example B — Volume for roadmap

Get: volume tier + prepay. Give: roadmap session and success hours.

Metrics

Leading: approval turnaround, posture adherence, % trades with offsetting gets.

Lagging: realized discount vs. target band, win rate, term.

Move from ad-hoc concessions to structured trades.

90-day rollout

Weeks 1–2 — Guardrails & posture

  • Owners: Pricing (lead), Finance, Legal, Sales Leadership.
  • Define discount floors, term bands, prepay rules and approval matrix.
  • Publish posture: firm items vs. tradeable items with ranges.

Weeks 3–4 — Build the menu

  • List common gives (discount, pilot, services, billing flex) with cost/constraints.
  • List matched gets (term, volume, prepay, reference, case study) with value.
  • Draft email snippets for requesting approvals with all data included.

Weeks 5–6 — Pilot & instrument

  • Use the worksheet on 8 live deals; measure posture adherence and approval time.
  • Dashboard: realized discount vs. posture, % trades with matched gets, approval SLA.

Weeks 7–8 — Enable & scale

  • Enablement sessions on trade language and framing; objection drills.
  • Legal “fast lane” for pre-approved trades (SLA 48h).

Weeks 9–10 — Optimize

  • Refine menu with new high-value gets (reference, logo usage, case study timeline).
  • Publish top 10 negotiation snippets that worked.

Weeks 11–12 — Bake into governance

  • Weekly deal desk checks: posture adherence and trade completeness.
  • Target: realized discount within band on ≥80% of wins; approval SLA <48h.
