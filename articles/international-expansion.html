International expansion: a practical playbook

Grow beyond your home market with a disciplined approach: decide where to play, validate quickly, localize what matters, then scale without losing control.

1. Decide where and why

  • Market signals: inbound demand, partner pull, competitive presence, regulatory barriers you can navigate.
  • Right to win: product fit, clear ICP, differentiated value versus local incumbents.
  • Operating cost & risk: payroll, taxes, legal, FX exposure, logistics.

Output: a one-page decision memo with target market, goals for the first 12 months, budget, and kill criteria.

2. Choose the entry mode

Pick one mode and commit for two quarters before you change it:

  • Direct sales hub: fastest learning, higher fixed cost.
  • Channel/partner-led: lower cost, slower feedback, needs strong enablement.
  • Distributor/reseller: simple footprint, lower control of brand and pricing.
  • Acqui-hire or JV: instant team and customers, highest integration risk.

3. Pilot and learn fast

  • 90-day pilot: 10–20 qualified meetings, 3–5 proposals, 1–2 signed customers.
  • Feedback loops: weekly pipeline review, bi-weekly win/loss notes, monthly pricing tests.
  • Guardrails: stop if unit economics or win-rate targets cannot be reached by month three.

4. Localize what matters

  • Offer: trim SKUs and features to a simple, priced bundle for the first six months.
  • GTM: rewrite messaging with local proof, update website pages, FAQs, and contracts.
  • People: hire one senior seller and one CSM, then add SDR or solutions engineer as needed.

5. Scale and consolidate

Once you hit repeatable win-rates and stable unit economics, start adding capacity. Standardize reporting and move from founder-led deals to a manager-led cadence.

Metrics and operating rhythm

  • Inputs: meetings set, partner activations, pilot users, content published.
  • Throughputs: win rate, cycle time, average deal size, NDR in cohort.
  • Outputs: ARR added, CAC payback, gross margin, time to first hire productivity.

Cadence: weekly pipeline, monthly market review, quarterly country plan refresh.
