Forecast Accuracy System

A practical weekly operating system to move accuracy from guesswork to evidence. No fluff. Ship it this week.

Outcomes

  • Quarterly forecast within ±5–8 percent of actuals.
  • Clear ownership on top deals and risks.
  • Leaders spend time coaching, not chasing numbers.

Foundations (definitions that stop arguments)

  • Expected Value (EV) = Amount × Stage Probability. Use EV for rollups; never sum raw amounts.
  • Stage probabilities: 10, 25, 40, 60, 80, 95 percent (adapt to your funnel). Document exit criteria in the CRM.
  • Commit classes: Commit (95 percent), Best Case (60–80), Pipeline (≤40). One deal, one class per quarter.

Hygiene rules (weekly)

  1. Every opportunity has next step, owner, date.
  2. Close dates are real (move when evidence changes, not when the quarter changes).
  3. Stalled logic: if no activity in 14 days, auto-flag as At Risk.
  4. Contacts: at least one economic buyer and one champion identified or explicitly missing.

Cadence

  1. Mon: Reps update hygiene before noon.
  2. Tue: Manager 1:1s (30 minutes) on top 3 EV + any red risks.
  3. Wed: Pipeline review (30 minutes). Use the template.
  4. Thu: Forecast call (20 minutes). Only rollups + risk register.
  5. Fri: Manager notes back to reps (coaching + asks to other teams).

Dashboards (build these, nothing else)

  • Coverage: EV vs. target by segment and owner.
  • Movement: Deals moved forward/backward by stage this week.
  • Stuck: >14 days in stage, by owner, with last activity.
  • Forecast trend: Weekly EV trend vs. actuals.

Inspection questions (leaders)

  • What changed since last week (evidence, not feelings)?
  • Who is the economic buyer and when did we meet them?
  • What is the single next step and by when?
  • What could kill this deal? What is our mitigation?

Risk register (simple but strict)

  • One line per deal: risk, owner, mitigation, date.
  • Review weekly on Thu. Close items or escalate.

Common failure modes

  • Stages are names, not definitions. Fix by writing exit criteria and locking fields in CRM.
  • Leaders debate top-line, ignore movement. Fix by using the Movement view weekly.
  • Commit is a wish. Fix by requiring economic buyer contact and a written mutual plan.

Checklist to deploy this week

  • Document stage exit criteria in your CRM.
  • Create four dashboards above and share links.
  • Enforce hygiene rules with a Monday reminder.
  • Run Tue 1:1s and Wed pipeline review from the same views.
  • Publish a one-page policy on Commit classes.

Keep it boring. Same rules, every week. That is how accuracy improves.
