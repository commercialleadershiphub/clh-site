Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Template

Dates, owners, risks and comms that keep deals moving.

TLDR

A MAP makes the plan visible on both sides. You list milestones from evaluation to go-live, assign customer owners, set dates, track risks, and review weekly. Deals move faster, surprises shrink, and decisions feel safe because responsibilities are shared.

Audience & situation

For AEs, AMs and CS leaders running evaluations or expansions with more than two stakeholders, legal/security steps, or a board date. If your opportunities stall late or slip month-to-month, start here.

Problem

Deals stall because only the seller has a plan. Dates slide, legal/security arrive too late, and executives don’t see clear ownership. Internal updates turn into guesswork instead of actions.

Why it matters

  • Predictability: cycle time variance drops when tasks have named customer owners.
  • Decision safety: executives see risk handled (security, data, procurement) before the ask.
  • Less thrash: weekly 15-minute reviews replace ad hoc chasing.

Pair this with strong discovery and crisp executive meetings to keep momentum.

Solution model

  • Milestones: evaluation start → security → legal → pilot → executive decision → contract → go-live.
  • Owners: seller + named customer owner for each milestone.
  • Dates & proof: target date and “definition of done”.
  • Risks: early flags with counter-moves and owners.
  • Comms: weekly 15-minute check-in; shared doc for notes.

Step-by-step guide

  1. Co-create on the first call: screen-share the MAP; fill owners/dates live.
  2. Front-load risk: add security/data/procurement steps early; attach intake links.
  3. Define proof: write acceptance criteria for pilot and decision checkpoint.
  4. Share & review: send link; hold 15-minute weekly review; update status and slips.
  5. Close with confidence: final read-out summarizes milestones met, risks closed, and remaining actions.

Example

Context: Mid-market fintech; security backlog; quarter end in 7 weeks.

  • Key milestones: security intake (Week 1), pilot kickoff (Week 2), exec read-out (Week 5), legal review (Week 6), sign (Week 7).
  • Risks: data access → counter: data owner assigned Day 2; legal capacity → counter: pre-approved LOA.
  • Result: MAP kept timeline visible; decision on Week 5, sign on Week 7 with no last-minute escalations.

Checklist or template

  • Milestone | Target date | Seller owner | Customer owner | Definition of done | Status | Risk/notes
  • Weekly notes: decisions, blockers, next actions, date moves (with reason).
  • Link to security/legal intake; add pilot acceptance criteria.

Co-create the MAP on day 1, then keep it honest with weekly 15-minute reviews.

Metrics & pitfalls

Metrics

  • Cycle time variance vs. plan
  • % milestones with named customer owner
  • Risk closure rate per week
  • Slips flagged ≥14 days ahead

Pitfalls

  • Over-engineering (keep to one page)
  • “We’ll fill it later” (fill it live on the call)
  • Owner drift (no unnamed milestones)

Implementation checklist

  • Publish a MAP one-pager and example notes; store in a shared library.
  • Add CRM fields for MAP active and Next MAP milestone; create a dashboard for slips and owner coverage.
  • Stand up pilot acceptance templates and legal/security intake links.
  • Block a weekly 15-minute MAP review with the customer during evaluation.

Measurement

Team level: MAP adoption on named deals, slip rate, average time between milestones, % risks with counters.

Individual level: % milestones with customer owners, forecast accuracy vs. MAP, timeliness of updates.

Team buy-in

  • Position the MAP as mutual risk management, not admin.
  • Demonstrate by co-creating one live; celebrate wins where MAP avoided a slip.
  • Keep it light: one page, short notes, weekly 15-minute cadence.

90-day rollout (extended)

Weeks 1–2 — Stand up the system

  • Owners: Enablement (lead), Sales Ops, Legal/Security.
  • Artifacts: MAP template, pilot acceptance criteria, intake links, dashboard spec.
  • Actions: add CRM fields and views; publish weekly review script; pick two gold-standard examples.
  • Exit: template approved; first 10 named opportunities using MAP.

Weeks 3–4 — Pilot

  • Run MAP on 10 opportunities across segments; review weekly; coach co-creation on calls.
  • KPIs: ≥80% milestones with customer owners; slips flagged ≥14 days ahead.

Weeks 5–6 — Instrument & coach

  • Dashboard live: adoption, slips, owner coverage, risk closure; publish two anonymized win stories.

Weeks 7–8 — Roll out

  • Make MAP mandatory on all strategic deals; add MAP status to pipeline review.

Weeks 9–10 — Tighten cross-functional path

  • Legal publishes LOA template; Security publishes a pre-check form; CS adds go-live checklist.

Weeks 11–12 — Bake into rhythm

  • Monthly audit of MAP quality; refresh examples quarterly.
  • Target state: cycle variance −15–20%; forecast accuracy +5 pts on MAP’d deals.

Next steps & CTA

  • Co-create a MAP on your next discovery or kickoff call.
  • Front-load security/legal and define pilot acceptance early.
  • Review weekly for 15 minutes; track slips and counters.
