Discovery Call Framework

Replace guesswork with a testable hypothesis and sharp questions that lead to a dated next step.

Introduction

Discovery is not a checklist. It is a test. You bring a hypothesis, ask questions that prove or falsify it and close with commitments that move the work forward. The goal is clarity, not charm.

This framework keeps prep short, the call focused and the follow-up crisp.

What good looks like

  • Hypothesis-led: we know what we are testing.
  • Questions with intent: each one earns a next step.
  • Commitments: both sides take actions with dates.
  • Recap ≤24h: in the buyer’s language with the next hold.

Playbook

  1. Write the hypothesis and call goals.
  2. Prepare questions by pillar (metrics, EB, criteria, process, pain, timeline).
  3. Agree the next step on the call and book the hold.
  4. Send a short recap with owners and dates.

Worked examples

Example A — Manufacturing ops

Hypothesis: overtime driven by scheduling variance; questions on drivers, cost and constraints; next step is a 2-week data check.

Example B — Retail IT

Hypothesis: change windows miss; questions on governance; next step is a sandbox challenge.

Metrics

Leading: recap rate, next step booked, call to next step days.

Lagging: stage progression, win rate, cycle time.

Hypothesis first. Questions second. Commitments before the call ends.

90-day rollout

Weeks 1–2 — Define the standard

  • Owners: Enablement (lead), Sales Ops.
  • Publish hypothesis template, question bank by pillar and recap email sample.
  • Add CRM fields for Hypothesis present, Recap sent and Next step date.

Weeks 3–4 — Pilot with 8 calls

  • Shadow and score discovery using the checklist; run two coaching clinics.
  • KPIs: recap ≤24h ≥80%, dated next step ≥85%.

Weeks 5–6 — Instrument & improve

  • Dashboard: recap rate, next step rate, days to next step, progression.
  • Publish top 10 questions that worked (with anonymized snippets).

Weeks 7–8 — Scale to team

  • Managers run weekly 15-minute “question drills”.
  • Introduce red-team on large first meetings (async review of hypothesis).

Weeks 9–10 — Tighten handoffs

  • SE/AE handoff doc for POC requests; CSM/AE handoff for renewal discovery.

Weeks 11–12 — Bake into operating rhythm

  • Deal review check: hypothesis, 3 best answers learned, dated next step.
  • Target: next step rate ≥85% sustained; cycle time −10–15%.
