QBR Playbook (Customer‑Facing)

Make QBRs useful for the customer and for you. One hour, clear outcomes.

Purpose

  • Confirm value delivered and align on next‑quarter outcomes.
  • Surface risks early and agree mitigations.
  • Earn the right to expand.

Prep checklist

  • Adoption and impact metrics (last quarter vs. plan).
  • Support tickets and themes.
  • Roadmap items relevant to the account.
  • Executive summary (one page) in plain English.

Data pack (send 48 hours before)

  • Usage by team and key workflows.
  • Business impact (time saved, cost avoided, revenue influenced).
  • Open risks and actions from last QBR.
  • Proposed objectives and measures for next quarter.

Agenda (60 minutes)

  1. 5 — Welcome and goals for the session.
  2. 15 — Outcomes delivered (show, do not tell).
  3. 20 — Risks and blockers (joint problem‑solving).
  4. 15 — Next‑quarter plan and success measures.
  5. 5 — Close: confirm owners, dates, and next touch.

Expansion plays (if fit)

  • Upsell tied to confirmed impact (not feature dumps).
  • Cross‑sell based on adjacent workflows.
  • Executive sponsorship exchange (you and customer).

Follow‑up (24 hours)

  • Send notes with actions, owners, and dates.
  • Update CRM fields and health score.
  • Book next QBR.

QBRs fail when they are demos in disguise. Keep it about outcomes.
