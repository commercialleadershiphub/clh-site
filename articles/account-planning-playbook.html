Account Planning Playbook

Turn intent into growth: clear objectives, a mapped committee, focused plays and dated milestones.

Introduction

Account planning fails when it becomes a slide exercise. Good planning is a monthly operating habit that aligns stakeholders, value and execution. This playbook keeps the artifacts light and the actions visible.

You will identify the business outcomes that matter, map the buying committee, write a value hypothesis, list risks that can derail momentum and commit to a small number of high-leverage plays. Then you will track milestones in the calendar and hold owners to dates.

What good looks like

  • One page: anyone can understand it in two minutes.
  • Owned dates: every milestone has a date, owner and hold.
  • Live risks: risks are worked each week, not parked.
  • Few plays: three good plays beat ten wish-list items.

Playbook

  1. Define the objective and value hypothesis.
  2. Map the committee and influence lines.
  3. List risks and mitigations.
  4. Choose 2–3 plays with proof and owners.
  5. Set milestones and calendar holds.

Worked examples

Example A — Expansion via reference architecture

Objective: standardize on our platform. Play: executive workshop with CIO and two BU leads; proof via reference architecture pack.

Example B — Renewal with growth

Objective: 24-month term + +15% seats. Play: value review + give/get (reference + term for price band).

Metrics

Leading: meetings with EB, milestone holds, play completion.

Lagging: expansion ARR, multi-product penetration, term.

Keep the plan short, the dates real and the plays few.

Implementation checklist

  • Publish the one-page template and the plays library.
  • Define “strategic account” criteria and assign owners.
  • Block monthly plan reviews in calendars and keep them timeboxed.
  • Add CRM fields for Milestone hold and Play status and test the dashboard.
  • Share two anonymized gold-standard plans as reference.

Measurement

Team level: expansion pipeline and ARR, sponsor coverage, milestone on-time %, plays started per account.

Individual level: plan hygiene, risk updates per week, next-step-dated compliance, quality of value hypothesis in reviews.

Team buy-in

  • Position the plan as a growth tool that creates focus, not extra admin.
  • Involve the team in play selection and publish wins to the library.
  • Celebrate when a plan unlocked an executive sponsor or an expansion.

90-day rollout

Weeks 1–2 — Stand up the system

  • Owners: Sales Ops (lead), Enablement, Managers.
  • Artifacts: one-page template, committee map, playbook library, milestone tracker.
  • Actions: publish templates; define “strategic account” criteria; add CRM fields for Milestone hold and Play status; create a monthly review cadence with calendar holds.
  • Exit: templates approved; 10 example plays documented; review cadence and dashboards visible to managers.

Weeks 3–4 — Pilot 5 accounts

  • Select five accounts across segments; assign an owner and a manager coach for each.
  • Run committee mapping and value hypothesis; write two risks per account and the first 2–3 plays.
  • Book one executive touch per account and log it against the plan.
  • KPIs: ≥80% milestones with calendar holds; ≥2 plays per account started; manager review completed for 100% of pilots.

Weeks 5–6 — Instrument & coach

  • Dashboard: milestone on-time %, EB meetings, play completion, risks worked.
  • Coaching: how to write a value hypothesis; “risk working”; how to choose high-leverage plays.
  • Publish anonymized examples of good plans; add a lightweight hygiene check to weekly 1:1s.

Weeks 7–8 — Roll out to all strategic accounts

  • Managers run monthly plan reviews; RevOps audits milestone hygiene and data completeness.
  • Introduce red-team review for big expansions (10-minute async) before proposal.
  • Share a plays library sorted by value driver to accelerate selection.

Weeks 9–10 — Tighten cross-functional path

  • CS + Sales agree expansion triggers and health signals tied to plays and risks.
  • Finance creates a fast path for term/volume approvals; pre-approve common scenarios.
  • Legal and PMM supply standard addenda and proof points to unblock deals.

Weeks 11–12 — Bake into rhythm

  • Add plan check to QBR: objective, plays, risks, milestones updated and owners confirmed.
  • Refresh examples quarterly; archive outdated plans; keep sponsor alignment current.
  • Target state: ≥70% of strategic accounts with executive sponsor; expansion pipeline +25%.
