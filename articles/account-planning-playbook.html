Account Planning Playbook

Roles, whitespace mapping and a practical 90-day plan for landing and expanding strategic accounts.

Problem

Most teams “plan” once a year, then chase deals. No clear owners, weak stakeholder maps, and no 90‑day execution rhythm. Result: low expansion and random acts of selling.

What good looks like

  • One page plan: goals, whitespace, stakeholders, risks, next 12 weeks.
  • Named owners: AE (net new), AM/CSM (expand/retain), Exec sponsor.
  • Monthly inspection: 30 minutes, same questions, visible progress.

Step-by-step

  1. Define objectives: revenue target, product adds, references.
  2. Map stakeholders: power, influence, gaps; assign outreach.
  3. Whitespace: product × BU matrix; flag easy wins and big bets.
  4. Plays: 3–5 specific motions with owners and dates.
  5. Risks & counters: competition, budget, politics.
  6. Cadence: weekly actions; monthly review with leadership.

Templates you can copy

  • One‑Page Plan: Objectives | Stakeholders | Whitespace | Plays | Risks.
  • Stakeholder Map: Name, role, power (H/M/L), stance, next touch.
  • 12‑Week Plan: Week, action, owner, proof of progress.

Metrics

  • Coverage: # of power stakeholders with next meeting booked.
  • Expansion pipeline: $ by product × BU; conversion to closed‑won.
  • Referenceability and executive touches per quarter.

Risks

  • Plans with no owners → convert to tasks per week, not vague goals.
  • Too many plays → lock to 3–5 and kill the rest.
  • Leadership drive‑by → set a recurring 30‑min review and keep receipts.
