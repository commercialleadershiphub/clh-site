Value Realization Reviews

EBR pack, KPIs and ROI narrative you can reuse.

Audience & situation

For CS leaders, AMs and Exec Sponsors. Use when quarterly reviews drift into status updates, value is implied not proven, and expansions stall because ROI is unclear.

Introduction

Great EBRs are short, numeric and reusable. They retell the original goal, show before/after evidence, and propose next steps with modeled ROI. The pack is built once and reused in internal forums (steerco, finance, procurement) so momentum compounds instead of resetting every meeting.

This playbook gives you a deck outline, a KPI/ROI rubric, and a repeatable meeting flow that ends with decisions—expansion pilot, executive sponsorship, or a focused improvement plan.

What good looks like

  • One narrative: goals → outcomes → proof → roadmap asks.
  • Two lenses: business impact (KPI delta, ROI) and user stories (adoption, quotes).
  • Clear close: expansion hypothesis, pilot scope, owners and dates.

Common pitfalls

  • Feature dump instead of outcomes.
  • Charts with no baseline/target.
  • No executive “ask”, so momentum dies.

Playbook

1) Prep the facts

  • Collect baseline vs. current for 3–5 KPIs tied to the original business case.
  • Pull usage/adoption to support the “how”.
  • Capture 2–3 credible quotes/screenshots as qualitative proof.

2) Build the deck (10–12 slides)

  • Goal recap (1 slide) with KPI table (baseline → current → target).
  • Before/after visuals (2–3 slides) with short captions and sources.
  • ROI page: assumptions, quick math, sensitivity.
  • Roadmap & asks: pilot scope, success criteria, timeline, owners.

3) Run the meeting

  • Open with the 90-second storyline; confirm the goal still matters.
  • Show proof; pause for validation or counter-evidence.
  • Propose options (A default, B phased, C no-regrets) and trade-offs.

4) Close & follow up

  • Summarize decisions, owners and dates; book the next milestone in-room.
  • Send a recap within 24h with links to data sources and the ROI sheet.

Artifacts

EBR deck outline

  • Goal recap & KPI table
  • Before/after evidence
  • ROI page (assumptions & sensitivity)
  • Roadmap & asks

ROI model

  • Volumes, rates, unit cost
  • Time saved and error reduction
  • Payback & break-even

Worked examples

Example A — Cycle time reduction

Goal: −20% approval cycle time. Proof: 14d → 10d (−28%). ROI: $220k/yr in labor + faster revenue recognition. Ask: expand to two additional regions.

Example B — Quality improvement

Goal: −25% rework. Proof: pilot line hit −27% with training + alerting. ROI: payback < 4 months. Ask: 3-site rollout; reference on pass.

Metrics

Leading: EBRs run with executive attendance, KPI tables completed, next milestone booked in-room.

Lagging: EBR→expansion conversion, payback on expansions, renewal uplift.

Implementation checklist

  • Publish EBR deck outline and two anonymized examples.
  • Stand up a shared ROI sheet with guardrails and sources.
  • Add CRM fields for Last EBR date and Next milestone.

90-day rollout

Weeks 1–2 — Define

  • Approve KPI rubric and deck outline; agree sources and owners.

Weeks 3–4 — Pilot

  • Run 5 EBRs; measure in-room decision rate; gather feedback.

Weeks 5–8 — Scale

  • Publish examples; coach storytelling; add ROI sensitivity patterns.

Weeks 9–12 — Bake in

  • Make EBR a renewal gate; track conversion; refresh quarterly.
