Sales Hiring Scorecard & Interview Loop

Hire for outcomes, not vibes. Keep the loop tight and comparable.

Role outcomes (define these first)

  • New ARR quota and ramp time.
  • Stage conversion targets and activity expectations.
  • Territory model and ICP focus.

Competencies (assessable, not buzzwords)

  • Prospecting: builds pipeline independently.
  • Discovery: finds pain and impact quickly.
  • Deal drive: creates momentum, manages process.
  • Writing & speaking: clear, concise, tailored.
  • Coachability and learning speed.

Interview loop (example)

  1. Screen (30 min): story, quota history, failures and learning.
  2. Panel (45 min): discovery role‑play on a real ICP problem.
  3. Deal deep‑dive (45 min): pick one deal; walk MEDDICC evidence.
  4. Writing task: a two‑paragraph email to an economic buyer.
  5. References: two managers, one peer, one customer if possible.

Scorecard (rate 1–5)

  • Prospecting
  • Discovery
  • Process & momentum
  • Communication
  • Coachability
  • Culture add

Decision meeting (30 minutes)

  • Each interviewer states score and hire/no‑hire in one minute.
  • Gaps listed with mitigation ideas.
  • Final decision and written rationale (store in ATS).

Anti‑bias guardrails

  • Same questions for all candidates.
  • Evidence over impressions. Use artifacts.
  • At least one interviewer from another team.

Document the loop. Train interviewers. Review outcomes every quarter.
